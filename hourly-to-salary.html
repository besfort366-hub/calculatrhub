Frequently asked questions

$25 an hour is how much a year?

$25 per hour × 40 hours per week × 52 weeks = $52,000 per year before taxes. Monthly that's $4,333, weekly $1,000 and daily $200 based on a standard full-time schedule.

$20 an hour is how much a year?

$20 per hour × 40 hours × 52 weeks = $41,600 per year gross. Monthly gross pay is approximately $3,467. After federal taxes for a single filer, take-home pay is roughly $34,000–$36,000 annually.

$30 an hour is how much a year?

$30 per hour working full-time (40 hours/week, 52 weeks) equals $62,400 per year before taxes. This is above the US median household income and represents a comfortable middle-class wage in most states.

How many hours a year is full-time?

Full-time employment in the US is typically 40 hours per week × 52 weeks = 2,080 working hours per year. If you take 2 weeks of unpaid vacation, that drops to 2,000 hours (40 × 50).

How do I convert salary to hourly rate?

Divide your annual salary by 2,080 (the standard full-time annual hours). For example, $52,000 ÷ 2,080 = $25 per hour. Adjust if you work different hours — divide by your actual annual hours worked.

What is the US minimum wage per year?

The federal minimum wage is $7.25 per hour, which equals $15,080 per year at 2,080 hours. However, many states and cities have higher minimum wages — Washington State's is $18.67/hr ($38,833/yr) as of 2026.
